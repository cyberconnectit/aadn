AMY'S ANGELS DAY NURSERY PARENT POLICIES AND PROCEDURES


Aim for the parent policies and procedures

The aim of these policies and procedures are to ensure a safe environment for children, staff and parents, whilst creating a welcoming and fun environment for all. Amy’s Angels Day Nursery prides itself on the highest standards of care, provision, policies and procedures as well as ensuring all staff members and parents at the setting follow all of these policies and procedures.

In Amy’s Angels Day Nursery there will be set rules of the policies and procedures and what to do in all situations relating to nursery as well as outside of the nursery to ensure your child is happy, learning at an appropriate level and are safe in as well as outside of the nursery.


Illness Procedures

AIM

The aim of these policies and procedures are to stop the spread of infectious diseases and to resolve a solution to any substantial outbreaks such as chicken pocks, measles, flu and any other illnesses that are contagious. As well as setting out when and how long you have to keep your child off nursery for if they have any illness.

• Amy’s Angels Day Nursery will follow the PUBLIC HEALTH (control of diseases) act 1984 legislation with best effectiveness and monthly reviewing’s.

• Any child with a temperature of and over 37.5oc should not be coming into nursery until the temperature has gone or returned to a safe level of 35.5oc. Any changes in a child’s temperature or continuous sickness or lack of appetite, parents should call 111 and seek medical advice immediately.

• If your child develops a temperature of or above 37.5oc you will receive a call asking permission to administrate medicine (Calpol) to your child and monitor their temperature for the next hour, if after 1 hour or during the hour your child temperature rises to an unsafe level medical assistance will be called and you will be informed immediately.

• If your child has sickness or diarrhoea, they must stay off nursery from 48 hours after the last symptoms.

• If your child has been given Calpol or any sort of medicine on the morning that they are set to come into nursery, the nursery should be informed of the medicine administered to your child and the reasons why, you will then be informed if your child is allowed to come into nursery or has to remain at home for the day.

• If any child in the setting comes down or has symptoms of chicken pocks then a letter will be immediately sent out to all parents alongside an email, informing you of the action we are taking to prevent further spread. If your child shows any symptoms of chicken pocks immediately receive doctor’s advice and intervention and inform the nursery at the most relevant time for yourself.

• If your child has any symptoms of the flu, measles, chicken pocks, conjunctivitis and any other contagious illnesses you should seek medical advice as well as informing the nursery immediately, so we can take action to prevent any further spread within the nursery as well as ensuring the most at-risk children (suffering with long term health issues) are safe and notified. And your child should be kept at home 48 hours after the last symptoms have passed and your doctor has cleared your child from all contagiousness.

• If your child comes into nursery with an illness, any staff member will refuse your child access until your child is better excluding (general illnesses such as colds, runny noses or normal cough).

• If at any point during the day we notice that your child is unlike themselves or come to us saying that they feel ill with a sore tummy, headache, sickness or any other symptoms then you will receive a call asking you to come and collect your child as soon as possible from nursery.

• When any contagious illness infects anybody in the nursery whether it being staff, children or parents the nursery will take necessary action to stop the spread and contain the contagious illness. When taking these necessary actions, the nursery may be forced to close for a short period of time.

• If your child has a long-term illness, Amy’s Angels Day Nursery will have the highest possible care if place so that your child is able to take part in all of the nursery’s routine and planning. We will also set up regular meetings with you to inform you of your child’s progress and any suggestions that either yourself or the nursery may have. We will also have highly trained staff in the room that can administrate any medication necessary.

Medicine procedures and administrations

Aim:

The aim of these policies and procedures are to ensure and relax parents about the administration of medicine and use of medicine at home. Our policies and procedures along with how we use medicine safely and document everything with witnesses when giving children any sort of medicine. We follow and ensure all of these policies and procedures are followed while as a nursery we follow the medicine 1968 legislation.

• Any medicine that you bring in for your child/ children without a doctor’s prescription we are able to administrate however we are only allowed to administrate the dosage on the bottle for the correct age as well as only for three days without a prescription then we are unable to administrate any more medicine to your child without a doctor’s prescription.

• Any medicine that is brought into the nursery whether it is prescribed by a doctor or by yourself, you will need to fill in and sign a medicine administration form. This is including every morning when you drop off your child so that we have in writing what time you administered the medicine at home if it is needed in the morning then again at the end of the day.

• Amy’s Angels Day nursery asks parents/ carers to provide Calpol that we can keep for your child in an emergency when they become ill with a temperature or any other illness, we are able to contact the first emergency person and get permission to administrate medicine to your child if needed.

• Any medication that is brought into the nursery regardless whether it is short term or long -term medication, a medicine administration form must be completed. If the medication is long term any changes in the medication or dosage you will need to inform your child’s keyworker and immediately complete a new medicine administration form.

• Any long-term medication that your child needs will be monitored and administered by your child’s key worker who will be well knowledged of your child’s health condition as well as undertaking training if needed on how to manage your child’s medication and condition.

• If yourself or a doctor prescribes medicine for your child more than 3 times in one month for your child which is continuous we may refuse to administer this medicine until we have spoken to your doctor with permission from yourself or main carer for your child. If declined, then Amy’s Angels Day Nursery will refuse to administer this medicine.

• All medicine will be given to the manager who will then store all the medicines with the room name and child’s full name locked away in a secure medicine cabinet in the manager’s office and can be accessed only by the manager who has a key. The child’s keyworker will be able to come and get your child’s medicine as and when it is needed to be administered.

• In any circumstances where any staff members think that the use of medicine is being abused by parents or carers they will immediately report these concerns to the Designated safeguarding officer and we ask that parents with any concerns do the same.

• If you have had to administer Calpol or any other form of medication to your child before coming into nursery this means your child is ill and therefore should not come into the nursery until the following morning or until the illness symptoms have passed. This is to stop the spread of infections.

Safeguarding children Policy and procedures

Aim:

The aim of Amy’s Angels Day Nursery safeguarding policy is to ensure the safety and welfare of all children in our care, giving all children the best possible outcomes in life where all children live in a suitable home free from abuse and are able to have the best start in life. The safeguarding policy and procedure are also in place to ensure staff and parents are aware of all signs of abuse and know what action to take in reporting any concerns to the nursery safeguarding officer or in absence of the safeguarding officer the Deputy safeguarding officer. We follow and use the following legislations in place, working together to safeguard children 2018 and the children Act 1989 (revised 2004)

• Any concerns raised by a member of staff will be confidential which therefore means you will be notified of the concerns and asked about them but the name of the person or persons who raised the concerns will be confidential.

• Any concerns raised to a member of staff will be taken seriously and with the upmost confidentiality and the first step will be to fill out a concern form and give back to the safeguarding officer.

• Concerns raised or brought to the safeguarding officer’s attention will be dealt with appropriately whether it’s a conversation with the parents or in a more serious concern is raised it will be taken to social services and the parents will be notified.

• If a concern is raised and the safeguarding officer believes that the child is in imminent danger, they will immediately call the police and social services without notifying the parents or carers of the child.

• In any situation where a concern is raised either by staff or parents where the safeguarding officer is unsure of what route or decision to make, then the safeguarding officer will contact our local LADO team which will give advice and support the safeguarding officer with the concern that has been raised.

• If you yourself as a parent has a concern that you wish to raise, then you can come into the nursery and speak to our safeguarding officer or Deputy safeguarding officer in confidentiality and then you will be expected to fill out a concerns form.

• Amy’s Angels Day Nursery is here to support all parents and children in every way including finding ways to stop abuse and to give all children the best start in life. We will always reach out to parents and support them in the best way we can but the children in our care are our number one priority and if a concern is raised, we need to take action.

• If you as a parent or family are struggling or looking for advice, the staff at Amy’s Angels Day Nursery will be more than happy to help and support you as a family/ parent especially your child’s keyworker and create a plan or solution to support both child and family as a whole.

• Anything discussed between parent or staff/ parent or keyworker will be confidential and will stay between parent and the staff member that has been told.

• If any issue or anything that has been told in confidentiality to a staff member which leads the staff member to have concerns or believe that either the parents or child are in danger, then this cannot be kept confidential and the staff member will be obligated to immediately file a concern to the safeguarding officer or Deputy safeguarding officer.

• All staff at Amy’s Angels Day Nursery are highly trained in safeguarding children and to recognise any signs of abuse as well as how to report these concerns. However, staff are also aware of all safeguarding policies and procedures.

• As a parent you have a responsibility to safeguard your child/ children and ensure that all their needs and requirements are met on a daily basis. If you are not meeting your child’s / children’s basic requirements, then this can and will be classed as neglect if you have not made any effort to reach out and get support.

Violence Policy and Procedures

Aim:

The aim of the violence policy and procedures is to ensure the safety and well-being of staff, other parents and children that are in the setting or attend the setting. We have a duty of care for our staff, children and parents are have a strong no tolerance policy for verbal or physical abuse towards any staff, children or parents on our premises and we ensure that everybody in our setting or around our building is safe.

• Any violence, verbal or physical abuse or rudeness towards staff, children or other parents will not be tolerated and there will be serious consequences or police intervention.

• If you are being violent in such a way where you are able to calm down as well as becoming a risk to yourself or anyone in the setting the police will be called immediately and Amy’s Angels Day Nursery or the victim may press charges.

• If you are verbally or physically abusive towards a member of staff, manager or owner on or off the premises you will receive a strike for this non-tolerable behaviour and this strike will be in writing and you could be called for a meeting.

• If you are demonstrating non-tolerable behaviour on a various or repetitive number of occasions, there will be consequences. Amy’s Angels Day Nursery have a strike system in place which will be further explained down below.

• If you are violent to any children on or near the premises this will be taken as a concern and it will be reported to social services immediately.

• If you are violent to any children on the premises to the point where a staff member or the setting believes the child or children are in imminent danger, we can refuse you access or to release the child/ children into your care and social services and the police will be called immediately. Amy’s Angels Day Nursery’s number one priority is and always will be the safety of the children.

• Any violence towards any other parent in or around the setting will also lead to a strike, depending on the severity of the offence Amy’s Angels Day Nursery will support the parent or victim in any way they can as well as supporting their claim if they decide to press charges on the offence or violence that was committed towards them.

• If any violence in the setting leads to bodily harm in any way this will lead to immediate police intervention, a meeting with both the owner of the setting, safeguarding team and if necessary and the severity of the incident the police.

• If the police are called out to intervene in a situation that you have caused this could lead to prosecution or charges pressed against yourself by the person that has been physically, verbally or mentally abused. If they decide to press charged the nursery will undertake a full investigation into this situation and give the victim full support.

• Amy’s Angels Day Nursery/ Nurseries have implemented the strike system to stop non-tolerable behaviour towards anyone in or around the setting because this type of behaviour is negative and should not be demonstrated around or near children of any age.

• If any of these offences are committed on any of the children that attends or are in Amy’s Angels Day Nursery, staff can refuse to release the children into your care or if they are not your children police will be called immediately so will the parents of the child and you could face a prison sentence of up to 4 or more years, as well as this if the parents of the child decide to press charges you could also have to pay a fine for compensation.

• If you commit an offence involving physical abuse on a staff member, parent or child we will be obligated to call the police and the police may make a decision to involve social services due to the injuries or bodily harm that you have caused.

Accident, incident and head injuries

Aim:

The aim for the accident, incident and head injuries policies and procedures are in place so that if your child/ children have an accident, incident or injury to the head while at nursery you are informed of all of this by staff who witnessed them. This policy is also in place so that the nursery can file and keep any accident, incident and head injury forms for up to 5 years after your child has left. These are also in place so the nursery can keep track of how accidents are happening which the owner can see, make a risk assessment and change certain aspects so that these accidents are not a regular occurrence.

• Any accidents, incidents or head injuries must be noted on the correct form by the member of staff who witnessed the accident at the first appropriate time after the child has been cared for by a qualified first aider.

• Any accidents, injuries or head injuries must be treated if necessary, by a qualified first aider and if they think it is necessary when your child has had an accident or incident you will be called immediately and notified of what’s happened.

• Any head injury whether it is a bump to the head or something different regardless of what’s happened you will be called by the witness after your child has been treated to inform you of what’s happened. If the first aider thinks so, then you will be asked to collect your child immediately and you could be recommended to take your child to the hospital.

• Head injuries are taken seriously with the upmost caution and for this reason we have a separate form for head injuries which have to completed by the witness of the accident as well as signed by the Deputy safeguarding officer, manager and by the parent or carer who picks up the child. You will then also receive a head care sheet and what to do if your child experiences any symptoms or unlikely behaviour.

• The nursery takes all accidents, incidents and head injuries very seriously and will always look into why this has happened and what can be changed to ensure that this never happens to another child.

• Amy’s Angels Day Nursery ensures that all members of staff working at the nursery whether they are full time, part time, bank staff or agency workers hold a full paediatric first aid title and feel confident to administer first aid in every possible situation.

• Children follow and learn from trial and error which means no matter how many risk assessments, supervision and procedures the nursery have in place children will always have minor accidents or head injuries, which we ask parents to be understanding about and follow the correct procedures which means signing any forms that are given to you.

• Amy’s Angels Day Nursery is highly equipped with all the first aid equipment that is recommended and more, we also have invested in a defibrillator in case a child goes into cardiac arrest to give the child the best chance of survival. As every second count in that type of situation.

• Amy’s Angels Day Nursery has first aid boxes located in all of the rooms full of every possible first aid equipment which is checked on a regular basis and filled when something is taken out of the first aid box.

• We ask parents that if your child has an allergic reaction to any equipment out of the first aid box that you notify the manger immediately and take photographic evidence if necessary, of the type of allergic reaction and co-operate fully to help us in undertaking a full investigation.

• Amy’s Angels DAY Nursery is also highly equipped with the number one recommended brand from professional doctors. We have the highest possible standards for all of our first aid equipment including a range of cold compresses.

External Injuries Policy and procedures

Aim:

The aim of the external Injuries policy is to ensure that no false accusations or referrals to social services are made when your child or children have an accident at home. To avoid this, we have set out this form so that any child or children that come into the setting with an injury no matter how small is recorded before you leave from dropping your child/ children off at the setting. Therefore, we ask parents or carers dropping children into the nursery inform the staff member that the child or children has an injury so that you can fill out the necessary forms.

• Any child brought into the setting with an external injury (injury that happened outside of the nursery), the parent or carer must inform either the child’s keyworker or member of staff of how the injury occurred and show them the injury immediately.

• The external injury form must be completed immediately before you leave the setting if this is not done, you could be called to either come back and complete the form or in some circumstances collect your child or children until an investigation has been completed into the accident and the appropriate forms not being filled out.

• When filling out the external injuries form you must ensure that you are clear with what happened to cause the injury on the child or children as well as who was present at the time of when the injury happened.

• At Amy’s Angels Day Nursery, we have high standards and pride ourselves on our parent and partner relationships which means that we expect this from parents and carers too, which means informing us of any injuries, changes to your child’s behaviour or any other things that you think the nursery should know so that we can best support your child and your family at the setting.

• If your child or children is having continuous accidents both at home and at the nursery, we will do a referral to a specialist to run some tests for some illnesses (such as dyspraxia, coordination delay and balance delay) which cause be causing your child to have accidents.

• Any refusals to run these tests could cause the nursery to raise concerns about the regular injuries which could lead to a multi- disciplinary team intervention with other health professionals such as a doctor and paediatrician to help the nursery get to the bottom of these injuries.

• The completed external injuries form will be stored once completed in your child’s file where it will remain for up to 5 years after your child has left the setting, after 5 years it will be disposed of correctly and with caution.

Observation and tracking policy

Aim:

The aim of the observation and tracking policy is in place so parents and carers understand the importance of the observation and tracking process. This is also in place so that parents can also be involved in observations and what role they can play.

• Observation and tracking play a very important role in your child’s/ children’s early years education as well as helping staff and your child’s keyworker to recognise what age and stage your child is at and how we can best plan and support you child.

• All members of staff in your child’s / children’s room will do observations on your child/ children and upload them onto our observation system. All observations will be linked to the EYFS and COEL.

• Once a member of staff has made and uploaded an observation this will then be used for tracking and then to plan personalised activities to best support your child in their development.

• Observations will be done planned and spontaneously as well as individually and grouped with other children.

• Parents can get involved in observations by sending the manager an email with a photo or video of your child hitting a milestone with a description of what your child is doing/ has done with the date on. This can then be uploaded onto our observations and used for tracking and planning.

• When you enrol your child or children into Amy’s Angels Day Nursery permission to allow staff members will be drafted for consent in your contract. However, observations are a legal part of nursery functioning and can be carried out without parents’ consent.

Fire Policy and Procedure

Aim:

The aim of the Fire policy and procedure is so that parents are aware of what to do if the fire alarm goes off when they are in the building.

• If you are in the building when the fire alarm goes off don’t panic, stay calm and you will be escorted out of the building as quickly as possible.

• If the fire alarm goes off find the nearest fire exit and assemble at the fire safety point, whatever you do don’t try to go into your child’s class and remove your child as staff will refuse and continue to evacuate the building.

• All parents and carers will be made aware of the Fire assembly point when enrolling and touring the Amy’s Angels Day Nursery. This is to ensure parents safety is a fire alarm ever goes off when they are in the building.

• If as a parent, you smell or see smoke while in or around the setting break the glass on the nearest fire alarm and set off the alarm. After this has been done evacuate the building as quickly as possible for your own safety.

• If you cannot find a way to evacuate the building in time and you become trapped in the building try to keep your mouth closed to inhale less smoke, try to bang or make sound with tools around you until help (firefighters) reaches you.

• Any fire drills are done throughout the day before pick up, therefore if a fire alarm goes off when in the building it would most likely be a real fire therefore you need to act quickly.
